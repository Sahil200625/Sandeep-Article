Social Media Awareness

Social media awareness is the understanding of how your actions on social media impact yourself and society as a whole. It involves being mindful of what you post, share, and consume on social media. It's also important to be aware of the security risks associated with social media. Here are some things to consider when using social media: What you post Be careful about what you post, as it can be difficult to control or delete, especially if someone else shares it. Avoid posting personal information like your address, birthdate, or social security number. Who you talk to Be aware of who you interact with on social media. Security risks Be aware of the security risks associated with social media, such as malware, social engineering attacks, account hijacking, clickbait, and scams. Employer considerations Employers may look at your social media presence when considering you for a job. Social media awareness can also refer to a marketing strategy used to increase brand awareness. Social media awareness campaigns can be a cost-effective way to reach a large audience and build trust with customers.

Evironment Awareness

Environmental education (EE) refers to organized efforts to teach how natural environments function, and particularly, how human beings can manage behavior and ecosystems to live sustainably. It is a multi-disciplinary field integrating disciplines such as biology, chemistry, physics, ecology, earth science, atmospheric science, mathematics, and geography. Self Awaereness

Self-awareness is the ability to focus on yourself and how your actions, thoughts, or emotions do or don't align with your internal standards. In philosophy of self, self-awareness is the experience of one's own personality or individuality. It is not to be confused with consciousness in the sense of qualia. While consciousness is being aware of one's body and environment, self-awareness is the recognition of that consciousness.
